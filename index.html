About

I am currently a M.S. student in the School of Electrical Engineering at KAIST and a member of the Autonomous Control & Stochastic Systems (ACSS) Lab.


My research aims to develop sample-efficient and trustworthy learning for robotic manipulation, enabling robot behaviors that are both rapidly adaptable and reliable in real-world environments. I focus on leveraging symmetry to improve data efficiency and on uncertainty quantification to support safe learning-based action planning, ultimately bridging low-level control and high-level decision making.
